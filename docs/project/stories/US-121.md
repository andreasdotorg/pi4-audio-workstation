## US-121: Simultaneous Multi-Channel Delay Measurement

**As** the audio system operator setting up at a venue,
**I want** to measure propagation delays for all speaker channels simultaneously using uncorrelated noise,
**so that** I can determine time alignment for 4 channels at once instead of running sequential per-channel sweeps, with delays updating in real time as I adjust speaker positions.

**Status:** draft
**Priority:** Medium — significant venue setup workflow improvement
**Effort:** 1-2 worker days
**Origin:** Owner-approved architect analysis (session 9)
**Depends on:** US-120 (real-time transfer function — shared infrastructure), US-052 (signal-gen — WhiteNoiseGenerator ready)
**Blocks:** US-122 (unified measurement display)
**Decisions:** D-040 (PW filter-chain), D-008 (delays as atomic matched set)
**Theory:** `docs/theory/multichannel-delay-measurement.md`

### Background

Current delay measurement requires sequential per-channel sweeps: mute all except one
channel, play sweep, record, repeat for each channel (20-60 seconds total, room must
be quiet). This story implements simultaneous measurement using per-channel uncorrelated
white noise and cross-correlation.

The key insight: when 4 channels each play a different uncorrelated noise signal, the
mic captures the sum. Cross-correlating the mic signal with each channel's reference
signal isolates that channel's contribution — the uncorrelated terms from other channels
mathematically cancel to zero. The cross-correlation peak position gives the propagation
delay; peak height gives relative level.

White noise (not pink) is optimal for delay measurement because its autocorrelation is a
sharp delta function, giving ~1 sample (20.8 microseconds at 48kHz) delay precision.

With 1 second of averaging at 48kHz (N=48,000), channel separation SNR is 47 dB — more
than sufficient for 4 simultaneous channels.

### Signal-Gen Changes Required

signal-gen currently sends the **same signal** to all active channels. This story adds
a `play_multi_noise` RPC command that assigns independent WhiteNoiseGenerator instances
(different PRNG seeds) to each channel. Seeds derived deterministically from channel
number (`seed = base_seed XOR channel_index`) so the reference signal can be reconstructed.

### Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] **AC #1: Per-channel multi-noise mode in signal-gen.** New `play_multi_noise`
      RPC command: `{"command": "play_multi_noise", "channels": [1,2,3,4], "level_dbfs": -20.0, "noise_type": "white"}`.
      Maintains up to 8 WhiteNoiseGenerator instances, one per channel, each with
      deterministic seed. Process callback generates independently for each channel.
      Each per-channel generator goes through SafetyLimits clipper.

- [ ] **AC #2: Cross-correlation delay finder.** Given mic signal and per-channel
      reference signals, compute cross-correlation to find propagation delay per
      channel. Peak position = delay (sub-sample interpolation for precision better
      than 20.8 us). Peak height = relative level at mic position. Updates
      continuously as averaging window slides.

- [ ] **AC #3: Simultaneous 4-channel measurement.** All 4 speaker channels measured
      at once. Delays converge to stable values within ~2 seconds of averaging.
      Channel separation > 40 dB (verified in local-demo with known asymmetric delays).

- [ ] **AC #4: Real-time delay tracking.** Delays update continuously as speakers
      are repositioned. Update rate: at least 1 Hz. Operator can watch delay values
      change in real time while adjusting speaker placement.

- [ ] **AC #5: Per-channel delay confidence indicator.** Each delay measurement
      includes a confidence level derived from the cross-correlation peak quality
      and per-channel coherence: **high** (Cxy > 0.85, sharp unambiguous peak),
      **medium** (Cxy 0.5-0.85, identifiable peak with noise floor), **low**
      (Cxy < 0.5, peak unreliable — do not use for time alignment). Confidence
      displayed alongside delay values. Low-confidence delays flagged with a
      warning and excluded from automatic time alignment application.

- [ ] **AC #6: Integration with transfer function engine.** Per-channel delays feed
      into US-120's time alignment — cross-spectrum computed with per-channel delay
      compensation, giving simultaneous per-channel transfer functions. Shared
      pcm-bridge streams (monitor for references, capture for mic).

- [ ] **AC #7: Safety compliance — combined level budget.** signal-gen -20 dBFS cap
      applies per-channel (S-2). Combined 4-channel simultaneous noise is +6 dB over
      single-channel (~-14 dBFS acoustic sum at mic position). The measurement gain
      profile (US-120 AC #5) must account for this +6 dB combined level — total
      measurement output must remain within D-009 safety bounds. Operator confirmation
      required before activating multi-channel noise (S-012). In design mode (US-120
      AC #4), Dirac room filters are loaded — signal still passes through crossover/HPF
      but without room correction attenuation.

- [ ] **AC #8: Local-demo validation.** Room-sim configured with different per-channel
      propagation delays. Multi-noise measurement recovers correct delays within
      1 sample tolerance (0.02ms at 48kHz). Secondary cross-correlation peaks visible
      for early reflections (validates acoustic crosstalk rejection). Confidence
      indicators converge to "high" in local-demo (no ambient contamination).

### Risks

1. **Nonlinear distortion cross-talk.** At high playback levels, speaker distortion
   products may weakly correlate with other channels' references. At -20 dBFS,
   distortion is negligible. Document level constraint.
2. **Long reverberation.** Venues with RT60 > 1 second add a diffuse floor under the
   cross-correlation, reducing direct-sound peak clarity. Mitigation: use short
   cross-correlation window focused on direct sound arrival.
3. **Sub-sample interpolation accuracy.** Parabolic interpolation of cross-correlation
   peak gives sub-sample precision, but accuracy degrades with low SNR. Verify
   precision with known delays in local-demo.
